Founder's Letter

We are not
here to help.
We are here
to build.

ML
Monaliza Lauren
Executive Director, Abide & Live Foundation
“Every person — regardless of gender, ability, or circumstance — deserves to command their own economic future. That is not a charitable sentiment. It is a structural demand.”

The communities we work with are not waiting to be saved. They possess land, labour, knowledge, and ambition. What they lack are functioning systems — reliable inputs at the right time, guaranteed buyers at fair prices, health infrastructure that doesn't collapse their economic gains the moment they start building.

Abide & Live Foundation was built to fix those systems. Not with pilot programs that never scale. Not with workshops disconnected from real markets. With precision, evidence, and the kind of institutional accountability that transforms one donor's confidence into a decade of lasting change.

This is Day 1 of something that will outlast all of us. Welcome.

Our Foundation

Five values.
No exceptions.

These are not aspirational. They are operational. Every program, every hire, every dollar is tested against these principles before it moves.

We are a market systems intervention platform with community health integration — built for institutional scrutiny, designed for lasting scale, anchored in evidence-first delivery from Day 1.

Dignity First
Every intervention preserves and elevates human agency. We reject the charity model, entirely.
Market Realism
We build enterprises, not dependencies. Sustainability comes from revenue — not repeated grants.
Radical Inclusion
Women, youth, and persons with disabilities are economic actors being systematically locked out. We unlock them.
Evidence Obsession
Every dollar generates data. Every program produces proof. We measure what matters and act on it.

Field Notes · Kenya, 2026
When the systems finally worked in her favour.

“A farmer doubles her income. Then malaria takes her out for three weeks at harvest. The gain evaporates. This is why we work at the intersection.”

Economic gains without health protection collapse at the worst possible moment. Our integrated model means the community health volunteer visiting your home is also your neighbour in the farmer cooperative. One system — not five programs running in parallel.

A woman earns more. Her children eat better. She stays healthy through harvest. She saves. She invests. She leads. That is not a program outcome — it is compounding prosperity. That is what we are here to build.

How We Work

How we actually deliver.

01

Pilot Fast, Learn Faster

Every program starts with a tightly-measured cohort. Baseline before anyone enrolls. Every dollar tracked against every outcome. No assumption survives first contact with the field.

02

Measure Everything

GPS-tagged, photo-verified, time-stamped digital data collection. Real-time dashboards. Third-party spot checks on 10% of data every quarter. No self-reporting without verification.

03

Kill or Scale

We don't pilot forever. If the unit economics don't hold, the program ends. If they do, the replication blueprint is already written. Test fast, scale smart, never romanticise failure.

04

Built for Scrutiny

Donor-grade governance from Day 1. All 15 core policies maintained and board-approved. Full due diligence package delivered within 48 hours of any funding conversation.
